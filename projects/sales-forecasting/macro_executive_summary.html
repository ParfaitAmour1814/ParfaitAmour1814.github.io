Macro Variable Regression
Overall Findings

Author: Truong Phat  |  Method: OLS (scipy.stats)  |  Period: Jan 2020 – Dec 2025  |  n = 72 months

Conclusion: All macro variables excluded from primary model

CPI YoY%, Interest Rate%, and GDP Growth% were tested individually and in combination against total monthly FMCG sales volume (2020–2025). None achieved sufficient explanatory power to justify inclusion. The combined model explained only 16.1% of variance (Adj. R²=12.4%), with multicollinearity between CPI and Interest Rate noted (CPI sign reversal in multiple regression). By contrast, Account 641 Marketing Spend tested across the same 72 months returned R²=17.0%, p=0.0003 — the strongest external variable found so far and statistically significant. This confirms that internal business levers (marketing spend, price, seasonality) explain FMCG sales better than macro indicators. Marketing spend will be evaluated for inclusion in the next model iteration alongside average selling price. A product-group level deep dive (Page 2) reveals important nuance in macro sensitivity across categories.

Methodology
Model
OLS via scipy.stats.linregress (simple) and sklearn.linear_model.LinearRegression (multiple regression)
Data
72 months (Jan 2020–Dec 2025). Sales: transaction data joined to item master. Macro: GSO Vietnam (CPI), State Bank of Vietnam (rate), World Bank/GSO (GDP quarterly → monthly)
Evaluation Metrics
R², Adjusted R², p-value, F-statistic, 95% Confidence Interval, MAE — at both total and product group level
Exclusion Criteria
Excluded if: R² < 0.15 individually, OR p-value > 0.05, OR combined Adj. R² does not meaningfully improve the primary model

Deep Dive Analysis · Product Group Level

Macro Sensitivity
by Product Group

Author: Truong Phat  |  Item master join: 92.1% match rate  |  Groups: 18  |  n = 72 months each

Why this matters: Total-level analysis masks important product-level behaviour. Disaggregating by NHÓM (product group from Oracle ERP item master) reveals that macro sensitivity correlates directly with product premiumness and purchase involvement. Powdered Milk (02XXXX) — often used for infant formula, a high-involvement category — shows significant sensitivity to all 3 macro variables (best R²=18.4%). Meanwhile Condensed Milk (01XXXX), a low-cost daily staple, is virtually immune (R²<1%). GDP Growth is the most consistent macro driver, significant in 8 of 18 groups — suggesting that consumer confidence and income levels matter more than price inflation or borrowing costs for FMCG dairy.

Strategic Implication
Most Macro-Sensitive Groups
02XXXX Powdered Milk — all 3 variables significant (GDP R²=18.4%). High-involvement purchase, infant formula segment. Consumer income and price levels directly drive brand/volume switching.

05XXXX Flavored Milk — CPI + GDP (R²=16.4%). Discretionary beverage, first to be cut in tighter economic conditions.
Most Inelastic Groups
01XXXX Condensed Milk — near-zero macro sensitivity (R²<1%). Low-cost daily staple consumed regardless of economic conditions. Price point and brand loyalty dominate.

07XXXX Other Dairy — similarly inelastic. Core household consumption category with predictable demand regardless of GDP or rate environment.
