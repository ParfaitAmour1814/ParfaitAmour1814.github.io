Research Output · Sales Forecasting Model

Macro Variable Regression
Executive Summary

Author: Truong Phat  |  Method: OLS Linear Regression (scipy.stats)  |  Period: Jan 2020 – Dec 2025  |  n = 72 months

Conclusion: All macro variables excluded from final model

Three macro variables — CPI YoY%, Interest Rate%, and GDP Growth% — were tested individually and in combination against monthly FMCG sales volume (2020–2025). None achieved sufficient explanatory power to justify inclusion. The combined multiple regression explained only 16.1% of variance (Adj. R² = 12.4%), with high multicollinearity risk between variables. This confirms that average selling price, seasonality, and trend are stronger predictors of FMCG sales volume than macroeconomic indicators — consistent with the inelastic demand characteristics of dairy and consumer staples.

Methodology
Model
Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) Linear Regression via scipy.stats.linregress (simple) and sklearn.linear_model.LinearRegression (multiple)
Data
72 monthly observations (Jan 2020 – Dec 2025). Sales: Vinamilk transaction data aggregated to monthly total. Macro: GSO Vietnam (CPI), State Bank of Vietnam (interest rate), World Bank/GSO (GDP quarterly → monthly interpolation)
Evaluation Metrics
R² (variance explained), Adjusted R² (multiple regression), p-value (coefficient significance), F-statistic (model significance), 95% Confidence Interval (coefficient range), MAE (prediction error)
Exclusion Criteria
Variable excluded if: R² < 0.15 individually, OR p-value > 0.05 (not statistically significant), OR combined Adj. R² does not meaningfully improve over single-variable models
